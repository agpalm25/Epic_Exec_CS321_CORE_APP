What does a CA do?

Role of a Community Advisor

The Community Advisor (CA) position at Colby College is a vital leadership role dedicated to creating a safe, inclusive, and supportive living environment in residence halls. CAs play an essential part in fostering community, building connections, and supporting the academic and personal growth of students.


Responsibilities

  • Community Development: Establish and maintain an inclusive environment where all residents feel welcomed, respected, and supported. Plan and execute programming, organize hall meetings, and help residents connect with each other.
  • Conflict Resolution: Mediate conflicts and facilitate roommate agreements proactively, helping to resolve any concerns in a constructive manner.
  • Resource Referral: Serve as a knowledgeable support system for students, referring them to on-campus resources such as counseling, academic support, and health services.
  • Duty & Emergency Response: Participate in a nightly duty rotation, conduct regular safety checks, and assist during emergencies, ensuring a safe environment for all residents.
  • Student Accountability: Uphold community standards and address inappropriate behaviors consistently. Work with professional staff to maintain a respectful, accountable community.

Training and Development

CAs undergo extensive training in leadership, community building, and emergency response. Training sessions occur at the beginning of each semester and prior to JanPlan. Attendance is mandatory to ensure all CAs are well-prepared to support their communities effectively.


Testimonials from CAs

Perks and Compensation

CAs receive an annual stipend of $5,000 and are assigned a designated single bedroom. This role offers invaluable skills in leadership, communication, and problem-solving, which are highly sought after by future employers.
